# Design

## Direction

Tensor Aurora is a cinematic mathematical workspace inspired by the clarity and
pacing of excellent educational animation. It does not copy another creator's
visual identity.

## Palette

- Deep navy-black scene background
- Chalk-white primary text
- Cyan for active tensors and navigation
- Gold for current mathematical focus
- Coral for negative values and warnings
- Muted blue-gray for supporting structure

## Typography

- Clear system sans for interface and explanations
- Monospace for shapes, values, coordinates, and formulas
- Strong but restrained hierarchy, with minimal uppercase labels

## Components

- Flat scene panels with crisp borders and subtle inset depth
- One active accent per control or visualization
- No decorative glassmorphism or broad purple gradients
- Buttons use clear filled or outlined states

## Layout

- Drawing, active scene, and explanation form the primary workspace
- Tablet uses two columns; phone follows draw, scene, explanation
- Timeline reads as a chapter rail and scrolls horizontally when needed

## Motion

- Motion personality is calm, precise, and cinematic.
- Every lesson scene follows setup, transformation, observation, and resolution.
- Guided playback synchronizes chapter pacing with each scene's internal simulation.
- Cyan traces data movement; gold marks the current mathematical conclusion.
- Scene changes use controlled directional transitions; controls use immediate feedback.
- No bounce, random floating, decorative pulses, or unrelated ambient movement.
- Reduced motion reveals final states immediately and disables guided autoplay.
